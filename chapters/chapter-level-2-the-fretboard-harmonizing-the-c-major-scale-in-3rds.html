The Fretboard: Harmonizing the C Major Scale in 3rds

Chapter objective. Play C Major in 3rds at a steady pulse and label each 3rd Major or minor while connecting the sound to the scale degree below it.

Retrieve first. Away from the diagram, say the Major/minor pattern of the first four 3rds in C Major. Then find C-E and D-F on one string pair.

Review point. This is the first Unit 1 performance task. Revisit it at the next practice session and during the Unit 1 checkpoint below.

In the last chapter you harmonized the C Major Scale in 3rds on paper. Now let’s put it on the neck.

First, the scale by itself

Here is the C Major Scale played straight up the A string, from the C at the 3rd fret up to the C an octave higher at the 15th fret.

Now add the 3rd above each note

In the previous chapter, we figured out how to harmonize the scale:

3 ➡ E F G A B C D E
1 ➡ C D E F G A B C

Now put it on the neck:

Every solid-line pair is the Major 3rd shape. Every dashed pair is the minor 3rd shape.

Exercise: Play it and Hear it

Here is how you would play it (in tab):

Practise once with four clicks per pair while saying the lower note and quality, then once with one pair per click at 60 bpm. Play ascending and descending.

Success standard. Pass after two consecutive up-and-down performances with a steady pulse, all eight pairs sounding clearly, and all eight qualities named correctly. If one pair fails, begin one pair before it and repair the transition rather than restarting from C every time.

Unit 1 checkpoint: 3rds

Close the book. Do these in one practice sitting, then check only after you have committed an answer or a take.

  1. Write the note-name span (quantity) and number of half-steps traversed (quality) of a Major 3rd and a minor 3rd.
  2. Create the 3rd interval starting on C, F, and G. What is the note that creates the 3rd?
  3. Create the 3rd interval starting on D, E, A, and B. What is the note that creates the 3rd?
  4. On the E-A string set, build a Major 3rd shape from one of these frets on the low E string: 1, 3, 8, 13, or 15. Then label the notes.
  5. On the E-A string set, build a Minor 3rd shape from one of these frets on the low E string: 5, 7, 10, or 12. Then label the notes.
  6. Play C Major, in 3rds, one pair, and say the quality of each pair as you play.
Pass rule and follow-up

Pass when the written answers are accurate, all six fretboard shapes contain the named notes, and the scale is steady with no more than one restart. If any item fails, repair that item, then try the whole checkpoint again at the next practice session. At the next three practice sessions, retrieve one random Major/minor 3rd before beginning new work.
