Music Theory: The Triad (A Bird's-Eye View)

Chapter objective. Construct a triad as 1-3-5 above any supplied scale degree and predict its quality from the two 3rds inside it.

Retrieve first. Build the 3rds above C, D, and E in C Major without looking. Keep those three pairs visible; they are the raw material for this chapter.

Review point. Triad construction is assessed in the Unit 2 cumulative assessment in Chapter 9.

In the last couple of chapters you harmonized the C Major scale in 3rds. Starting on any scale note and counting up to the note a 3rd above gave you an interval pair. However, a pair of notes is not quite a chord. A chord is generally 3 or more notes (the Power Chord is the one exception). But we need one more note to make a 3-note chord and we can find that note with the formula: 1-3-5.

Triads

A triad is a three-note chord constructed by stacking intervals of a third. You build a triad exactly the way you harmonized the scale, just carried one step further. Take a note, add the note a 3rd above it, then add yet another note a 3rd above that. The result is three notes, each one a 3rd from the next.

1 – 3 – 5

This is the bird's-eye view. Every chord in the key of C is one of these seven stacks, and each is built by the same simple rule: a root note (any note in the scale), plus every other note above it, twice. Or think: 1-3-5.

Hear it

You have been building stacks on paper; now sound one. The very first stack, C-E-G, sits under your fingers on three neighboring strings:

  • C on the A string, 3rd fret
  • E on the D string, 2nd fret
  • G on the open G string

Fret the first two, then strum just those three strings and let them ring together. Three notes at once. That full, settled sound is the I chord you just built from the 1-3-5 formula.

Try it yourself

Cover the finished table and spell the triad built on each of these roots, using only the C Major scale and the 1-3-5 formula. Remember to loop back to the beginning of the scale when you run past B.

  2. A
  3. B
Answer key
  • E: E-G-B (the iii chord)
  • A: A-C-E (the vi chord)
  • B: B-D-F (the vii° chord)
